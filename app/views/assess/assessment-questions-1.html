Consider:

  • If there is an immediate risk of harm, including risk to self, which an AP placement can support in managing.
  • Whether an AP placement would still be necessary if alternative accommodation was available. AP placements are not solely for accommodation purposes even for a person who is considered high risk. Consider suggesting whether CAS3 may be more appropriate. If you believe this to be an application for accommodation only, you must clearly explain why.

Note that whilst there should be reference to the need for AP residence in the OASys Risk Management Plan, this may not be fully formed at the point of a timely application. An application should be made 6 months prior to arrival which in many cases will be during the OMiC handover period.

Consider which features of an AP are essential or beneficial for risk management. This could include monitoring and control measures that an AP can offer, such as:

  • Curfews & sign ins
  • CCTV
  • Room searches
  • Rehabilitative activities (formerly purposeful activities)
  • Monitoring of medication
  • Monitoring of risk to self
  • Monitoring of mental health needs

The application should include an explanation of how these measures would add value to the management of the risk of serious harm.

If the AP environment and facilities would not provide added value to the management of risk, consider whether an AP placement is appropriate.

Consider the location of any victims, local associations, and/or exclusion zones which may affect placement.

Provide details of areas or locations which may be unsuitable.

Considerations regarding location may include:

  • Risk to the person's children
  • Any permitted family contact
  • Domestic violence situations where the person is still in a relationship with a victim. Are there licence conditions or other orders which prevent contact or overnight leave?
  • Whether placing the person in an alternative area would disrupt a pattern of offending or associations with criminal peers
  • If there are partner agency factors to take into account when placing. For example joint agency management such as MAPPA or the police
  • Other orders such as restraining orders or sexual harm prevention orders (SHPOs) which may provide additional exclusion zones or location restrictions, such as distance from schools or playgrounds.
  • Media and local community reactions which may affect placement
  • Whether an AP in an area of racial and ethnic diversity is required

Positive factors
Identify if there are positive factors such as support from family, friends, other agencies and/or employment that would make placement in a specific area beneficial to the person.

At a minimum the application should contain a description of the intended actions the probabtion practitioner will undertake to support a timely move on. For example, referrals to local housing providers which can be followed up in the initial 3-way post arrival within 5 working days.

There is no requirement for a concrete move on plan or address to be supplied as some accommodation providers will not allow referrals to be made this far in advance.

Foreign Nationals
Foreign Nationals without recourse to public funds or employment may be dependent on Home Office provided accommodation for move on. These circumstances will be addressed post release. However, there should be evidence that the applicant has made contact with the case worker to have an early discussion regarding move on options.

Move on date
Consider whether the expected move on date provided is sufficient for the aims of the AP placement to be achieved. And alternatively, whether it significantly extends beyond that which is required.
